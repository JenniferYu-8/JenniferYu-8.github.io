Ad Hoc

Committee Description

An ad hoc committee is a specialized and impromptu forum that assembles to address immediate and unforeseen international issues or crises. Unlike the regular MUN committees with predetermined agendas, ad hoc committees replicate real-world diplomatic challenges requiring rapid response, creative problem-solving, and swift adaptation. Delegates participate in high-intensity sessions where they must be well-informed and adept at diplomacy, navigating evolving global scenarios while collaborating with others to craft effective solutions. This unique MUN experience tests their crisis management skills, making it an exciting and educational component of CANMUN.


Special features about Ad Hoc:


  1. No position paper needed!
  2. You will not know what the committee is about until your first committee sessions!
